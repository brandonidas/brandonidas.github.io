Let me introduce myself!

I'm a Computer Science undergraduate at the University of British Columbia. I TA for Computer Science 110 (Systematic Program Design) and 210 (Software Construction with Java) - and love it! (See fig.2, fig.3) I enjoy puzzles and thinking through proofs - which drew me to Computer Science.

Lately, I've been thinking about the *domain* of the puzzles I want to solve in my lifetime, which has led to start thinking about a second major in a Life Science. Pharmacology? Biochemistry? Proteomics? They all seem so ripe with complexity and ready to be computationally reaped!

I'm a very proud former member of the Military Medicine Institute (Singapore). I did not think much of myself prior to my time there and I have my commanders to thank for uncovering a lot of the better parts of who I am today. (See fig.2)

Buses 'R' Us, Bus Android App, for CPSC 210 coursework
Not a lot can be said about this one...

The code was not allowed to be published as the project was reused.

Nonetheless I can say that I used a public transit API to retrieve both the timings and variable routes of buses in British Columbia (yes bus routes in BC change seasonally especially around UBC). Then mapped these onto an a street map of the area.

It was meant an introduction to indsutry work (via a real world API and app), Object Oriented Programming and Design Patterns (Observer, Composite and Iterator).
